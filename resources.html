Crisis & Emergency Contacts

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 911. For mental health or substance use crises, contact one of the following:

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline 988 Call or text 988 — 24/7 support for mental health crises, suicidal thoughts, and substance use emergencies.
Crisis Text Line Text HOME to 741741 Free, 24/7 text-based crisis support. Text from anywhere in the US.
CT BH Access Line (BHCARE) 1-800-674-8278 Connecticut's behavioral health access and crisis line. Available 24/7 for mental health and substance use crises.
SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357 Free, confidential treatment referral and information service for mental health and substance use disorders. 24/7, in English and Spanish.
NAMI Helpline 1-800-950-6264 National Alliance on Mental Illness helpline. Information, referrals, and support. Mon–Fri, 10am–10pm ET.
Emergency Services 911 Call 911 for immediate safety emergencies involving imminent risk of harm to self or others.

Naloxone (Narcan) — Opioid Overdose Reversal

Naloxone (brand name Narcan) is a safe, effective medication that can reverse an opioid overdose. It is available without a prescription at most pharmacies in Connecticut.

For training on how to recognize and respond to an opioid overdose, contact the Prevention Office or visit DMHAS Naloxone Resources ↗.
