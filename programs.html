Elevate WeHa

Community wellness & mental health promotion

Elevate WeHa is a community-wide wellness initiative that works to promote mental health, reduce stigma around behavioral health challenges, and build a more connected, resilient community. The initiative brings together residents, local businesses, schools, faith communities, and Town departments to normalize conversations about mental health and increase awareness of available resources.

Elevate WeHa supports programming across the lifespan — from youth mental health awareness campaigns to resources for seniors experiencing isolation. The initiative aligns with the State of Connecticut's public health goals and serves as an umbrella for several Prevention Partnership efforts.

Focus Areas

  • Mental health awareness campaigns and community education
  • Reducing stigma around seeking help for mental health and substance use
  • Building community connectedness and social support networks
  • Partnering with schools, faith communities, and local businesses
  • Promoting available local and statewide behavioral health resources

Able Table

A sub-initiative of Elevate WeHa, Able Table brings together community members around shared meals in a welcoming, accessible environment. The program is designed to reduce social isolation — a key risk factor for both mental health challenges and substance misuse — by creating intentional opportunities for connection across generations and backgrounds.

Able Table events are open to all West Hartford residents. Scheduling and locations are announced through the Prevention Partnership's email list and social media.

PHACTT

Parents Helping Adolescents & Children Through Tough Times

PHACTT is a parent education and support program designed to help families navigate the challenges of adolescent mental health, substance misuse, and related issues. The program provides parents and caregivers with knowledge, tools, and community connection to better support their children through difficult times.

PHACTT sessions are led by trained facilitators and create a confidential, non-judgmental space for parents to share experiences, ask questions, and learn evidence-based strategies for communicating with adolescents about risk behaviors.

What Parents Learn

  • How to talk with teens about substance use, mental health, and peer pressure
  • Recognizing warning signs of substance misuse or mental health struggles
  • How to access local treatment and support resources
  • Strategies for setting boundaries while maintaining connection
  • Self-care for parents and caregivers under stress

Who Should Attend

  • Parents and guardians of children and teens in West Hartford
  • Grandparents and extended family members with caregiving roles
  • Educators or community members working with youth

YLA

Youth Leadership Academy

Teen leadership & prevention advocacy

The Youth Leadership Academy (YLA) is a youth development program that trains West Hartford high school students to become peer advocates for healthy, substance-free living. Participants develop leadership skills, public speaking confidence, and a deeper understanding of mental health and substance misuse prevention.

YLA participants work alongside Prevention Partnership staff to plan and implement community education campaigns, participate in town-wide events, and represent the student voice in local prevention efforts. Completion of the program is recognized by the Town of West Hartford.

Program Highlights

  • Leadership development and public speaking training
  • Education on substance misuse, mental health, and brain development
  • Peer-to-peer prevention campaigns at local schools
  • Community service and civic engagement opportunities
  • Mentorship from Prevention Partnership staff and community volunteers
  • Letter of recognition for college applications and resumes

SG

SMART Groups

Self-Management and Recovery Training support groups

SMART Recovery is a science-based program for people seeking to manage addictive behaviors, including alcohol and drug use. Unlike 12-step programs, SMART uses cognitive behavioral tools grounded in research to help participants build and maintain motivation, cope with urges, and build a balanced lifestyle.

SMART Groups facilitated through the Prevention Partnership are free, open to West Hartford residents, and held in a confidential, supportive setting. No prior experience or commitment is required — participants may attend as many or as few sessions as they find helpful.

SMART Recovery Tools

  • Building and maintaining motivation to change
  • Coping with urges and cravings
  • Managing thoughts, feelings, and behaviors
  • Living a balanced lifestyle in recovery

RFC

Recovery Friendly Community

West Hartford's commitment to supporting residents in recovery

West Hartford has committed to being a Recovery Friendly Community — a designation that reflects the Town's belief that recovery is possible, and that everyone deserves a community that supports their journey. This initiative works to reduce stigma, increase awareness of treatment and recovery resources, and build a culture of belonging for individuals and families affected by substance use disorder.

The Recovery Friendly Community initiative involves collaboration between Town government, local businesses, schools, healthcare providers, and community organizations. Together, they work to ensure that West Hartford is a place where no one facing addiction needs to face it alone.

What It Means to Be Recovery Friendly

  • Training for local businesses on how to support employees in recovery
  • Reducing stigma through public education and awareness campaigns
  • Connecting individuals and families with treatment and peer support resources
  • Advocating for policies that support recovery at the local level
  • Creating visible, welcoming spaces for people in recovery throughout the community
