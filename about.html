About Us

The people and groups behind prevention work in West Hartford.

PP

Prevention Partnership

Community collaborative — open to all

The West Hartford Prevention Partnership is a collaborative community group that brings together residents, organizations, schools, businesses, faith communities, and Town staff to promote mental health and prevent substance misuse in West Hartford.

The Partnership meets monthly and is open to any community member who wants to be involved in prevention efforts. It serves as the primary vehicle for community engagement and program implementation.

What the Partnership Does

  • Plans and implements community-wide prevention programs and events
  • Connects residents to local and statewide resources
  • Raises awareness about mental health and substance misuse prevention
  • Coordinates with schools, healthcare providers, and community organizations
  • Supports the Prevention Council's advisory work

PC

Prevention Council

Town-appointed advisory body

The West Hartford Prevention Council is an appointed advisory board that reports to the Town Council. Council members are appointed by the Town Council and serve staggered terms. The Prevention Council advises on local substance misuse prevention policy and strategy.

The Prevention Council also provides oversight and direction to the Prevention Partnership's programmatic work, and supports the efforts of the Prevention Coordinator and Social Services Department.

Role of the Council

  • Advises the Town Council on substance misuse prevention policy
  • Provides governance and direction to the Prevention Partnership
  • Reviews and recommends prevention programs and resource allocations
  • Monitors local substance use trends and needs
  • Advocates for prevention resources and policy at the local level
